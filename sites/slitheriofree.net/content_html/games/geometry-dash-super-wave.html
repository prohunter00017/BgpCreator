Mistake 1: The "Oopsie-Daisy" Jump

You know that feeling when you're flying through the wave and suddenly—BAM!—you hit a barrier? Yeah, that's called the "Oopsie-Daisy" jump. It happens when you get too excited and forget to control your wave. To fix this, try practicing with slower waves first. Get a feel for when to press and release the button. Imagine you're a bird soaring through the sky—smooth and steady wins the race!

Mistake 2: The "Speedy-Gonzales" Rush

Ever feel like you're in a race with a cheetah? That's the "Speedy-Gonzales" rush. You press the button so fast that your wave goes haywire. Take a deep breath and remember: slow and steady wins the wave. Try counting to three before you press the button again. It'll help you stay in control and avoid those pesky barriers.

Mistake 3: The "Forgotten-Floor" Flop

Oops! Did your wave just hit the floor? That's the "Forgotten-Floor" flop. It happens when you're so focused on the barriers above that you forget about the ground below. To fix this, try playing with a friend. They can remind you to keep an eye on both the ceiling and the floor. Two heads are better than one, right?

Mistake 4: The "Frustrated-Quit" Quit

Feeling frustrated? Don't let the "Frustrated-Quit" quit get the best of you. Remember, every wave master started as a beginner. Take a break, have some fun with friends, and come back with a fresh mind. You got this!
