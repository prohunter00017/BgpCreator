What Makes Blast Processing Special?

Blast Processing stands out with its 3D embellishments that make the game look super cool. You'll also see the wave shape in long, uninterrupted sequences, which adds a whole new level of challenge. This level is all about timing and precision, so get ready to dash hard and glow brighter!

Two Tricky Spots to Watch Out For

There are two tricky spots in Blast Processing that you should be aware of:

  • After the twin wave section, if you bypass the deactivation dual portal, the second cube part will start upside-down. But don't worry! You can still finish the level by quickly jumping after entering the cube portal and leaping over the blocks.
  • Right after the first ball segment, when you re-enter the wave form, tapping or clicking right after entering the wave portal will skip the gravity portal. This lets you use anti-gravity to sail through the wave section, dual wave, and part of the cube section.

Find Those Secret Coins!

There are three hidden coins in Blast Processing that you can collect to boost your progress. Here's where to find them:

  1. The first coin is at 52%, and you get it while in wave form. Enter the third enormous diamond after passing through the dual portal.
  2. The second coin is at 69%, and you can grab it while in UFO mode. Navigate through the "mouth" of the third major barrier towards the ground, just after passing under the second large face obstacle.
  3. The third coin is at 86%, and you collect it while in ship form. Look for the cube with artificial spikes and slide beneath the obstruction to get the coin.

Fun Trivia

Did you know that Blast Processing introduces artificial slopes as part of collecting the first hidden coin? These slopes aren't fully shown until the Theory of Everything 2 level. Also, the level isn't named after its Newgrounds song, which is titled "-BlastProcess-," but in other places, the song is called "Blast Processing."
